We Come to You and We Kill Ticks

Diseases caused by insect infestations are on the rise. Human caused environment landscape change (building roads and towns), climate change, and shifts in predator populations have contributed to this.

Ticks in particular are deadly. Until the scientific community comes up with real solutions which will take years, municipalities are at risk of disease epidemics.

Ticks spread disease. They look for deer, dogs, humans, any mammals, suck their blood, and the process get people sick. We don't like ticks or other malicious-parasites. That is why we like to kill them.

The average American deer is no Bambi. They are strong, they are infested with insects, and they walk around all day spreading diseases and ticks. One of the strange side-effects of the rather recently created American suburbs (less than 100 years), is that deer populations have exploded due to an improved living and eating climate. Without consecutive extremely harsh winters (we can't control the weather yet) or an army of vigilante deer hunters (I would rather not), deer populations will continue to explode. Curtailing deer populations has a direct effect on curtailing deer-tick populations.
